谈论未来科技

1. 词汇—有关未来科技的词汇

I—video

Simon: That was an amazing. documentary.

Jake: Gotta love those tiny robots.

Simon: Yeah. But I find it hard to believe these robots will go inside our bodies, find cancer cells and destroy them.

Jake: I don’t know - some of the predictions about future technology really blew me away.

Simon: I liked when the scientists predict we’ll have genetic scanners that can read our whole DNA in two minutes. But I find it hard to believe.

Jake: I think, in the future, robots will be everywhere.

Simon: Yeah. We’ll have robot babysitters, robots helping the elderly …

Jake: No! I mean these robots will be everywhere, and they’ll be so small, we won’t be able to see them. They’ll get inside our brains and tell us what to do. Must buy that brand of soda. Must have those new sneakers.

Simon: Seriously, though, it’s shocking how much robots will take over our lives in the future.

Jake: Scary, huh? Oh. I have another prediction.

Simon: What?

Jake: I predict … a pizza delivery.

Simon: Good one.

II—单词

cell

robot

human body

technology

medical scanner

DNA

III—选择正确的词

Some day [robots bodies scanners] will take care of children and the elderly.

If there are [technology cancer elderly] cells in her body, the medical scanner will tell us.

He got his [cancerous medical robotic] degree at Johns Hopkins University.

In the future, robots will destroy cancer [scanners elderly cells] .

The [DNA robot cancer] in our body has all of our genetic information.

2. 表达—预测

I—notes

预测

1>. 使用情态动词 will 和 be going to 预测将来要发生的事情。

Robots will take care of the elderly.

Doctors are going to have medical scanners they can hold in their hands.

2>. 你可以加上 think that，表述你猜想将来会发生的事情。

I think that robots will do more of our work.

3>. 你也可以使用动词 predict 来做出预测。

Scientists are predicting that fewer people will die from cancer.

4>. 使用表达 in the future 强调你在谈论离现在很远的一个时间。

-In the future, people will wear their technology, not carry it.

II—连线

Robots will drive around the body.

In the future, robots will take care of people.

Scientists are making predictions about robots.

I predict robots will repair cells.

3. 表达—表达惊讶或疑问

I—notes

表达惊讶、惊奇或疑虑

1>. 你可以说 That’s surprising! 和 That’s amazing! 来表示惊讶或惊奇。以下是其他一些表示惊奇的表达：

That medical scanner blew me away.

I can’t believe it!

Shocking!

2>. 你可以说 I doubt it 来表示疑虑。以下是其他一些表示疑虑的表达：

I find that hard to believe.

That’ll never happen.

4. 听力—注意说话者的看法

I—notes

聆听说话人的态度

人们谈论将来时，可能会持有许多不同的观点。

1>. 当人们态度乐观时，他们会使用意义积极肯定的词汇。

I’m very optimistic. It’s going to be amazing! Friendly robots will do our work, and life will be easier.

[我非常乐观，未来会充满惊奇！友好的机器人将承担我们的工作，生活将更加轻松。]

2>. 当人们态度悲观时，他们可能表达疑虑，使用意义消极否定的词汇。

I’m pessimistic. I doubt that we can ever stop cancer. It’s a scary illness, and we’ll never know enough about it.

[我持悲观态度。我认为我们不可能治愈癌症。这种疾病非常可怕，我们永远也不能充分了解。]

3>. 人们在不肯定时会表达 uncertainty（不确定性）。

I’m not sure. I really don’t know what will happen.

[我不确定。我真不知道会发生什么事情。]

4>. 表达疑虑时，说话人也可以使用表示可能的情态动词，比如 may、 might 或 could。

Robots might help us, but the environment could be a problem.

[机器人可能帮助我们，但是环境可能是个问题。]

II—dialog(听力资料)

REPORTER: Excuse me, ma’am. Could you answer a question for The News Daily?

WOMAN: Sure.

REPORTER: What do you think the future will be like ?

WOMAN: Well, I recently read an article about DNA. Scientists have created a computer made of DNA. Scientists will put the computers in our bodies. The DNA computers will help give us medicine, which is pretty cool. But the computers could attack our bodies. That’s scary ! So, I guess that I don’t know if the future will be good or not.

III—听音频、录音频

The future is going to be amazing!

I’m very pessimistic.

I feel optimistic.

I really don’t know what will happen.

I have a positive attitude about the future.

I’m not sure.
