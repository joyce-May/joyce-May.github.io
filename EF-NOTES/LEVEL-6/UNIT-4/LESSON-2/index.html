参加社交活动

1. 词汇—对话话题

I—notes

对话话题

努力避免谈及诸如politics、religion、money或romance之类可能引起强烈反应的话题。相反，选择你认为对方可能感兴趣又不会触犯对方的话题。

A: What kind of sports do you like?
B: I’m a big soccer fan.
A: Did you see the game last night?
B: Yes! It was really exciting, wasn’t it?

A: Do you enjoy traveling?
B: Yes, I do. I went to India last year. I loved it!

A: The weather‘s nice right now, is’t it?
B: Yes, it is. I love the summer here.

II—听音频、录音频

He won’t talk about money with people he doesn’t know.

I like talking about weather, sports and family.

No politics - people can’t agree on the president.

How about work - you’re an engineer, aren’t you?

She feels uncomfortable talking about her religion.

I enjoy talking about food and travel.

My boss never talks about romance. It’s too personal.

2. 口语—闲聊寒暄

I — notes

闲聊寒暄

使用这些话题在排队和社交活动上进行简短而礼貌的对话。这称作“闲聊”。

Wow - it’s so hot outside!

This event is very well-planned.

The food’s not too bad.

So, what do you do for a living?

I’m with Crump and Company. Where are you?

Are you here with family?

Are you talking any time off this summer? (休假)

II—dialog

Linda: Hello. I’m Linda Torres.

Billy: Hi, Linda. I’m Billy. Billy Green.

Linda: Nice to meet you, Billy. Great event, huh?

Billy: Yeah, it’s really well-planned.

Linda: Right. And the food’s not so bad.

Billy: True. So, what do you do?

Linda: I’m in sales, for LivingStore.com.

Billy: Oh, really cool company.

Linda: Yeah, Living Stone’s great. Where are you?

Billy: I’m a VP at Gardeb Love.

Linda: Wonderful! So you stay busy, right?

Billy: Very busy! But I’m taking some time off to see Athens while I’m here. How about you?

Linda: No, I’ve got to get right back to the office. So, are you here with family?

Billy: No, my wife had to stay home with kids, unfortunately.

Linda: Oh, that’s too bad. Hey, let me introduce you to my husband, Frank. He’s right over there. He can give you some excellent advice about Athens. He’s a travel agent.

Billy: OK. Sounds good.

III—听音频、录音频

Are you taking any time off this summer?

Are you here with family?

This event is very well-planned.

Wow - it’s so hot outside!

I’m with Crump and Company. Where are you?

The food’s not too bad.

So, what do you do for a living?

3. 表达—找出共识

I—video

Nina: Haven’t we met somewhere before?

Woman: No, I don’t think so.

Nina: Oh. Sorry.

Nina: So, are you from around here?

Woman: Oh, not really. My husband and I moved here a few weeks ago.

Nina: Oh, really? So did I. I think the area’s lovely.

Woman: Oh, me, too. We’re going to open a book shop down the road in about a month. You should come to our opening.

Nina: I will. Thanks. So what did you do before?

Woman: Oh, well, I worked at Crow and Dunny’s. Do you know it?

Nina: Crow and Dunny’s! I used to work there! I knew you looked familiar.

Woman: Oh, right. Did you know Rickard Moat?

Nina: Oh, yes. Isn’t he the worst? I know everybody who worked for him hated him!

Woman: That’s my husband. We just got married.

Nina: Oh! Oh, oh, not that Richard Moat! There was a … oh, what was his name? Richard … Richard Moon … Richard Mooney?

II—笔记

找出共识

1>. 与刚在社交活动上见到的某人建立共识是有好处的。如果你认为你以前见过对方但不肯定，使用类似表达。

We’re met somewhere before, haven’t we?

You look familiar.

2>. 使用类似表达确定你是否来自同一地区：

Are you from around here?

Have you always lived in the area?

3>. 使用类似表达确定你是否知道同一人物或地点：

I was the manager at Zutz Bakery. Are you familiar with it?

Did you ever work with Robin Meyer?

III—连线成句

Are you familiar with Zutz Bakery?

Haven’t we met somewhere before?

Are you from around here?

Do you live in the area?

You look familiar.

Did you ever work with Tiffany?
